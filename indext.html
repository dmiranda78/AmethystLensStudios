WHERE STORMS SPEAK AND SHADOWS TELL STORIES

Inspired by Nature

My work captures the haunting beauty of moody, storm-driven landscapes and the raw presence of portraiture steeped in the tones of early photography. Blending nature's turmoil with human truth, each image echoes the atmosphere of a forgotten time, revealing what lies just beneath the surface.

Drawn to Storms

Capturing the raw emotion of storm-lit skies, windswept landscapes, and the quiet drama of nature's darker moods. Amethyst lens is drawn to contrast, mystery and moments that most overlook. Whether it's the quiet before the storm or the electric chaos of a thunderstorm, we immortalize the poetry of the weather.

Portraits

My portrait photography captures the soul in moments of stillness—where raw emotion meets the weight of atmosphere. Inspired by the heavy skies of stormy weather and the somber grace of late 1800s to early 1900s imagery, my work leans into darkness, not to obscure, but to reveal. Each portrait is infused with the textures of antique toning, echoing the visual language of early photographic processes—sepia, silver gelatin, and wet plate ambrotypes.

I seek not perfection, but presence—those quiet, brooding instants where light barely clings to the face, where emotion drifts like clouds over a landscape. Gloom, grain, shadow, and subtle defiance—all become part of the frame. This is portraiture that speaks in a hush, that lingers, that remembers.
